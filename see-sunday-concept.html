See Sunday
before Sunday.

First visits are nerve-wracking. So before you ever walk in — walk in.

Let's get the awkward part over with.

Where do I park? What about my kids? What do I wear? Will I fit in? How long is it?
…or just walk me through it all ↓
The concept

A guided, first-person walk-through of a Sunday — built for the nervous first-time guest. It answers the questions people are too embarrassed to ask out loud, before they ever set foot in the building.

Opening move: name the emotion (nervous) in sentence one, then hand over control — they can jump straight to their specific worry instead of being forced through a linear tour.
Tip: use the toggle (top-right) to hide this commentary and see it exactly as a guest would.
01

You found us.

4570 Mackinaw Rd. A big lot, easy in and out. Roll up about ten minutes early and you'll have your pick of spots.

Easy in, easy out
The intent

The first real unknown is logistics — where to go and whether they'll look lost pulling in.

Fear it kills: "Where do I even go?"
Design move: a concrete address + "arrive ten minutes early" gives them a plan, not a guess.
02

Which door?

Easy — just walk toward the people. Head for the main doors and a greeter's there to point you exactly where to go.

Someone's expecting you
The intent

The wrong-entrance moment is a real first-visit dread. A promised greeter means no one walks in confused.

Fear it kills: "What if I pick the wrong door?"
Design move: "walk toward the people" — one human instruction, zero map-reading.
03

Coffee's on us.

Grab a cup, take a breath, look around. No badge, no spotlight — just slip in, get comfortable, and be exactly who you are.

No one will single you out
The intent

Lowers the social temperature. The #1 first-visit fear is being singled out — so we name it and kill it outright.

Fear it kills: "Everyone will know I'm new."
Design move: coffee gives their hands something to do so they're not standing there exposed.
04

Your kids are safe.

Secure check-in: your child gets a name tag, you get the match, and only you can pick them up. They'll have a blast — you'll actually get to listen.

Matched, secure check-in
The intent

For parents this is make-or-break. Concrete secure check-in detail earns trust fast.

Fear it kills: "Are my kids actually safe here?"
Design move: lead with safety, then the payoff — "you'll actually get to listen."
05

Sit anywhere.

Jeans, hoodie, whatever you've got on — come as you are. Sit wherever you like. No pressure to sing, stand, or be anyone but you.

Come as you are
The intent

Answers "what do I wear" and "what's expected of me" in one breath, and removes performance pressure.

Fear it kills: "Will I stick out or do the wrong thing?"
Design move: "come as you are" — permission, not rules. No obligation to sing or stand.
06

The next hour or so.

A few songs — sing along or just take it in. A quick welcome. And a message that's real talk you can actually use by Monday.

About 75 minutes, start to finish
The intent

Demystifies the unknown middle — what happens, in what order, and for how long.

Fear it kills: "Will I be trapped or lost in rituals I don't know?"
Design move: a concrete runtime (~75 min) + "useful by Monday" reframes the message as relevant, not preachy.
07

When it ends.

Want to talk, pray, or just grab info? Stop by and say hi. No pressure, no chase — the next move is always yours.

The next move is always yours
The intent

Handles exit-anxiety — the fear of being cornered or pressured on the way out.

Fear it kills: "Will they chase me to come back?"
Design move: "the next move is always yours" — control stays with the guest.
08

We saved you a seat.

That's it. No surprises — just show up, and we've got the rest.

We're in your corner.
